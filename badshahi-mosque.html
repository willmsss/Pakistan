Badshahi Mosque

A Mughal masterpiece and one of the world's largest mosques, standing in the heart of Lahore.

A Monument of Mughal Grandeur

The Badshahi Mosque (Royal Mosque) was built in 1673 by the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb and is one of the most iconic symbols of Pakistan. Located in the Walled City of Lahore, it faces the Alamgiri Gate of the Lahore Fort across a vast open courtyard, forming one of the greatest architectural ensembles in the Islamic world.

The mosque can accommodate up to 100,000 worshippers — 55,000 inside the prayer hall and on the portico, and 95,000 in the courtyard. Its three marble domes and four minarets rise above the Lahore skyline in quiet majesty.

Visitor Information

The mosque is open to non-Muslim visitors outside of prayer times. Visitors must remove shoes and women must cover their hair. Entry is free of charge. The mosque museum houses relics attributed to the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).
