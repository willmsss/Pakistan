Travel Tips for Pakistan

Everything you need to know to plan a safe, enjoyable, and memorable visit.

Before You Go

Visa & Entry

Many nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival or an e-visa online. Check the National Database and Registration Authority (NADRA) portal for current eligibility.

Health & Vaccinations

Recommended vaccinations include Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid, and routine immunisations. Carry a basic first-aid kit and any prescription medications.

Currency

The Pakistani Rupee (PKR) is the local currency. ATMs are widely available in cities. Carry some cash when travelling to remote regions.

SIM Cards

Local SIM cards from Jazz, Telenor, or Zong can be purchased with your passport. Mobile data is affordable and widely available.

Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly, especially outside major cities; cover shoulders and knees
  • Remove shoes before entering mosques and many homes
  • Use your right hand for eating and giving/receiving items
  • Greet with "Assalam-u-Alaikum" (Peace be upon you) — it is warmly received
  • Ask permission before photographing people
  • During Ramadan, avoid eating and drinking in public during daylight hours
  • Alcohol is restricted — be aware of local laws

Pakistani Hospitality

Pakistanis are renowned for their warm hospitality. If invited to a home, it is customary to bring a small gift such as sweets or fruit. Refusing food offered by a host is considered impolite — accept graciously and eat a little even if not hungry.
