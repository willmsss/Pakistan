Tourism Economy

How tourism is transforming Pakistan's economic landscape.

According to the World Travel and Tourism Council, travel and tourism contributed directly and indirectly approximately US$14 billion to Pakistan's economy, making up around 2.7% of the national GDP. In just two years, tourism grew by over 300% as security improved and international recognition increased. The Pakistani government set a target of Rs. 1 trillion in tourism revenue by 2025, reflecting the sector's growing importance to the national economy.

According to the World Travel and Tourism Council, the direct contribution of travel and tourism to Pakistan's GDP in 2016 was Rs. 793 billion, constituting 2.7% of total GDP. By 2025, the government predicted tourism would contribute Rs. 1 trillion (approximately US$3.6 billion) to the Pakistani economy.

Tourism Growth Drivers

Adventure Tourism

Five of the world's 14 eight-thousanders are in Pakistan, drawing climbers and trekkers globally.

Heritage Tourism

Six UNESCO World Heritage Sites and thousands of historical monuments attract cultural tourists.

Eco-Tourism

Diverse ecosystems from the Thar Desert to mountain glaciers support growing eco-tourism initiatives.

Improved Security

Significant improvements in security since 2014 have enabled rapid growth in international arrivals.
