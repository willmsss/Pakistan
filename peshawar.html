Peshawar

One of South Asia's oldest cities — the gateway to the Khyber Pass and Pashtun culture.

The Frontier City

Peshawar is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in South Asia, with a history spanning over 2,500 years. It served as the capital of the ancient Gandhara civilisation, was part of the Achaemenid Persian Empire, and was later influenced by Greek, Buddhist, Mughal, Sikh, and British cultures.

Today it is the capital of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province and a centre of Pashtun culture, famous for its bazaars, chapli kebab, and the Qissa Khwani (Storytellers') Bazaar.

Top Attractions

Peshawar Museum

Renowned for its collection of Gandharan Buddhist sculpture, one of the finest collections of its kind in the world.

Mahabat Khan Mosque

A 17th-century Mughal mosque known for its exquisite frescoes and polychromatic fretwork on the minarets.

Qissa Khwani Bazaar

The historic "Street of Storytellers" — one of Asia's oldest bazaars, renowned for its tea houses and merchant culture.

Khyber Pass

The famous mountain pass linking Pakistan to Afghanistan, steeped in history as a strategic gateway and trade route.
