Lahore

Pakistan's cultural capital — a city of Mughal grandeur, vibrant bazaars, and legendary food.

The Heart of Pakistan

Lahore is the capital of Punjab and the cultural, artistic, and educational heart of Pakistan. With a history spanning over a thousand years, it was the capital of the Mughal Empire at the height of its power and retains some of the most magnificent Mughal-era architecture in the world.

The Walled City of Lahore, containing the historic bazaars, mosques, and havelis (mansions), is a UNESCO World Heritage area that has been continuously inhabited for over 2,000 years.

Top Attractions

Lahore Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the fort covers 20 hectares and features 21 monuments from multiple Mughal emperors.

Badshahi Mosque

One of the world's largest mosques, it can accommodate 100,000 worshippers and is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture.

Shalimar Gardens

A Mughal garden commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1641, featuring three terraced levels with fountains and pavilions.

Tomb of Jahangir

The grand mausoleum of the Mughal Emperor Jahangir, located in Shahdara and displaying intricate marble inlay work.
