Getting Around Pakistan

A guide to trains, buses, airports, and metro systems for travellers.

How to Travel in Pakistan

Pakistan has a growing and improving transportation network connecting its cities, regions, and tourist destinations.

By Air

Pakistan International Airlines (PIA) and several private carriers connect major cities. Key airports include Jinnah International (Karachi), Islamabad International, and Allama Iqbal International (Lahore).

By Train

Pakistan Railways connects most major cities. The Green Line and other express services offer comfortable long-distance travel. The Lahore–Karachi route is a classic journey.

By Bus

Daewoo Express, Faisal Movers, and other companies operate comfortable inter-city coaches. They are often cheaper and faster than trains on many routes.

Metro Systems

Lahore and Islamabad–Rawalpindi have modern metro rail systems. Lahore Orange Line Metro is Pakistan's first driverless metro, cutting travel time significantly.

Rickshaws & Taxis

Auto-rickshaws are ubiquitous in cities. Ride-hailing apps like Careem and inDrive also operate widely, offering safe and convenient urban transport.

Jeep / 4WD

For northern mountain regions like Hunza, Skardu, and Fairy Meadows, renting a jeep or 4WD is the most practical option.
