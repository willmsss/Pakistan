Fairy Meadows

A magical plateau at the base of Nanga Parbat — one of earth's most dramatic landscapes.

Where Earth Meets the Sky

Fairy Meadows — known locally as Joot — is a lush, flower-filled plateau at an elevation of 3,300 metres in the Diamer District of Gilgit-Baltistan. It sits directly at the base of Nanga Parbat (8,126 m), the ninth-highest mountain in the world and the western anchor of the Himalayan range.

On clear days, the views of Nanga Parbat's Rakhiot Face — a sheer 4,600-metre wall of ice and rock rising from the meadow — are simply overwhelming. At night, far from any city, the Milky Way is visible with extraordinary clarity.

Travel Information

Best Season

May to October. The jeep track and footpath are inaccessible in winter due to heavy snowfall.

Getting There

Drive from Islamabad to Raikot Bridge on the Karakoram Highway (~16 hrs), then a jeep track to Tato Village, then a 3-hour hike to the meadow.

Accommodation

Several basic but charming wooden guest houses and camping sites available. Book in advance during peak season.

Trekking

From Fairy Meadows you can trek to Nanga Parbat Base Camp (2–3 days round trip) through pristine wilderness.

Altitude Warning

Fairy Meadows sits at 3,300 metres. Allow one day to acclimatise before trekking higher. Drink plenty of water and ascend slowly.
